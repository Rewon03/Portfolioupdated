I want you to redesign my personal portfolio website. Before making any design decisions, understand the distinction between my content source and my design inspiration.

My current portfolio website contains the complete content that must be preserved. It represents my personal and professional journey, my biography, education, experiences, projects, achievements, skills, interests, and overall identity. The content on my portfolio is not meant to sell a service, generate leads, or function as a business landing page. It is a digital representation of who I am. Think of it as a living autobiography, a professional archive, and a personal story combined into a single website. Everything that makes up my journey should remain present and discoverable.

However, while my current website contains the correct content and structure, it does not fully represent my design taste, personality, or aesthetic preferences.

For visual inspiration, design language, typography, spacing, atmosphere, mood, color palette, component styling, hierarchy, and overall experience, I want you to use the Ahrefs website as the primary design reference.

The goal is not to recreate Ahrefs as a company website.

The goal is not to transform my portfolio into a SaaS landing page.

The goal is not to make visitors think I am selling software.

Instead, I want you to imagine a scenario where the Ahrefs design team was hired to create a personal website for me. The content remains entirely personal, but the execution, sophistication, and visual identity should reflect the same design maturity, confidence, and premium quality found throughout the Ahrefs website.

The final experience should feel intelligent, modern, calm, confident, and highly intentional. It should feel like the website belongs to someone who values technology, creativity, design, knowledge, and thoughtful craftsmanship. The site should communicate professionalism without feeling corporate. It should communicate personality without feeling casual. It should feel polished without becoming flashy.

One of the things I admire most about Ahrefs is that it feels incredibly confident. It does not try too hard to impress visitors. It simply presents itself with clarity, strong hierarchy, beautiful typography, and exceptional visual balance. I want that same emotional feeling throughout my portfolio.

When someone lands on my website, I do not want their first impression to be "This is a portfolio."

I want their first impression to be:

"This person has a clear identity."

"This feels carefully designed."

"This feels premium."

"This feels memorable."

"This person has depth."

The typography should play a major role in achieving this feeling. Use large, bold, expressive headings with a clear hierarchy similar to Ahrefs. Headlines should feel important and impactful. Supporting text should be readable and elegant. Avoid small typography that makes the site feel crowded. Allow typography to create rhythm and visual interest throughout the page.

The spacing philosophy should be heavily inspired by Ahrefs. One of the strongest qualities of the Ahrefs website is its generous use of whitespace. Every section feels intentional because content is given room to breathe. I want the same approach applied throughout my portfolio. Avoid cramped layouts. Avoid stacking too much information together. Create visual pauses between ideas. Let the content feel important by giving it space.

For colors, use a dark and premium visual system inspired by Ahrefs. The primary background should be a deep navy or near-black blue rather than pure black. Text should maintain strong contrast and readability. Accent colors should be used carefully and intentionally. Orange may be used as a primary accent because it reflects the confidence and energy of Ahrefs, but it should never dominate the design. The color palette should feel mature, refined, and timeless rather than trendy.

The navigation should be simple and elegant. It should help visitors explore my story rather than navigate a product. Every section should feel like a chapter in a larger narrative about who I am and how I have evolved over time.

The hero section is extremely important.

I do not want a traditional portfolio hero section that simply says "Hello, I'm Rewon" followed by a list of skills.

Instead, create a hero section that feels powerful and memorable. It should immediately communicate identity, curiosity, ambition, and personality. Large typography should dominate the space. The hero should create emotional impact before presenting details. The feeling should be closer to a premium editorial experience than a conventional portfolio template.

The About section should not feel like a resume summary. It should feel like storytelling. It should communicate who I am, how I think, what drives me, what I value, and how my interests have evolved. The design should encourage visitors to spend time reading rather than skimming.

The Education and Experience sections should be transformed from ordinary resume-style layouts into visually engaging narratives. Present milestones as part of a journey. Show growth, progression, learning, and evolution. The design should help visitors understand not only what I have done, but how I arrived there.

The Projects section should receive special attention. Projects should be displayed using premium card designs inspired by the level of polish found in Ahrefs. Each project should feel like an important piece of my journey rather than just another item in a portfolio grid. Use strong hierarchy, beautiful spacing, subtle interactions, and clean presentation. Projects should feel meaningful and curated.

The Skills section should avoid cliché progress bars or generic percentage indicators. Instead, present skills through thoughtful categorization and elegant visual organization. The goal is to communicate expertise while maintaining a sophisticated aesthetic.

I also want sections that reveal personality beyond professional achievements. Include areas that can showcase interests, current explorations, creative pursuits, favorite ideas, technologies I am learning, subjects I enjoy studying, or philosophies that influence my work. These sections are important because they help visitors understand the person behind the accomplishments.

Motion and animation should be subtle, smooth, and purposeful. Avoid flashy effects. Use motion to support storytelling and create delight. Elements should feel alive but never distracting. Every interaction should contribute to a sense of quality and refinement.

The design system should feel consistent throughout the entire experience. Buttons, cards, navigation elements, content blocks, section headers, timelines, project displays, and contact elements should all belong to the same visual language. The website should feel like a cohesive product rather than a collection of unrelated sections.

Responsiveness should be considered from the beginning. The desktop experience should feel spacious and immersive, while the mobile experience should maintain the same elegance, hierarchy, and personality without sacrificing readability.

Most importantly, remember that this website is not trying to sell anything.

It is trying to represent a person.

My existing portfolio contains the story.

Ahrefs contains the visual personality.

Your task is to merge these two worlds.

Preserve the depth, authenticity, and personal nature of my portfolio while elevating the design quality to the level of Ahrefs. The final result should feel as though my life story, professional journey, achievements, projects, interests, and personality have been presented through a world-class design system.

The ultimate design goal is to create a website that feels less like a portfolio and more like a beautifully designed digital autobiography—one that reflects both who I am and the design aesthetic that resonates with me.
